## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->
This week was another towing the line of burnout, but with vacation on the horizon I was accepting of the much needed rest. I spent the week battling between the past and future. The war was symbolized by the indecision between two color schemes I'd paid to have made for my personal brand: one of calm, nature-inspired themes and the other rustic and more "manly." I'd been opening up at how little I perceived growth in others, which opened a wormhole into my self-perceived resilience and growth. Doing so brought out alot of insecurity and alot of OCD: I spent a majority of Friday in a sleep-deprived and anxious state where I couldn't remember if I'd turned the stove off and gotten to witness the onslaught of anxious thoughts which reminded me of the kind I used to have as a kid. Then Sunday, I'd spent an entire hike self-concious about my use of trekking poles on a "popular" hike with distracted hikers. I'd ended the week beginning to understand that perfect doesn't exist and I've wasted too much time trying to make it. I've attached many identities to my present circumstances (eg barista, poor, living at home, etc), evaluated my attractiveness on the opinions of "easy" women, and struggled to stand out because of bullying in middle school. Selling my guitars and cleaning out the basement has become a testament to letting go of the past so that I may make room for who I'm meant to be. I leave this week with an optimism, I'm still exhausted and in need of a vacation, but I feel big changes are coming soon.
