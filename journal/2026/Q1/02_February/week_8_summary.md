## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->
Began the week fretting over the intoxicating swirl of pleasure. Most of my contemplations were on the nature of attraction and how possesive obsession has always been my core desire. I relapsed and obsessed over this until we received a cancer diagnosis for Nana. The most accurate quote: "You know it's always about not being able to tolerate the real world and so I had this place to retreat where I was safe and I was wanted and I was necessary and I had attention. But now it's getting to a point where I'm strong enough to withstand life and so it feels like there's no purpose to holding onto it because I don't need it as a retreat or a safety or security anymore." Getting further into the week, I realized the connection between reprising identities and the decluttering I'd undertaken: we hold onto identities in the same way we hold onto objects. We think one day we'll need them again, even when it's been years. Selling off old trinkets has opened up the identities I've held myself to: the bullied kid and the owned. Letting go of these has grown my confidence. And in doing, I've become more intimately aware of the connection between net worth and the value provided for others. That in order to make more, more value needs to be provided to the community. Likewise, we become great by placing ourselves into scenarios which expect a higher valued output, and doing our best to live up to those standards.
