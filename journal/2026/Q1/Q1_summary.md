## Summary

<!-- Summarize the quarter in 1000 characters or less. -->
The focus of the months in this quarter have been in redirecting my coping mechanisms. Having started the year with quitting masturbating, video gaming, and being on social media left me withdrawn from social environments and coping mechanisms. I tried substituting the social experience with attending jazz jams and run clubs, which led me to playing at my first jazz jam, running my first 15 miles, and then making ~10 new friends at both events. January was an uphill battle against the addictions themselves, while February and March were struggles against the ways I'd used them to cope. Overeating, overspending, and obsession filled in the gaps where those things used to lie. Once my glasses broke, everything spiraled for a while. I was unable to attend the jazz jams because I couldn't see at night and then run clubs had a similar reasoning, though I'm convinced now I was just embarassed and scared. Still, many good things came out of this period. I finished paying off my last interest-generating credit card and decluttered all of the basement and bedroom, creating a dedicated practice/rehearsal space which permits recording. March continued the coping spiral of February, but moreso because of the increased confidence than anything. Whereas I'd allowed others to make my opportunities for me before, March came with the realization that I was responsible for leading the life I wanted to live. So I planned/executed a camping trip with my family, got (eye) doctors appointments, and started a consistent practice with Jalen. These all shed light on latent insecurities as I realized a large part of leadership is exposing your vulnerabilities and dealing with them then and there, which in turn exposed the way I'd coped with them previously as being self-destructive. Leaving the quarter intending to change my systematic habits for dealing with these pains so that I'm no longer afraid of facing them.

A final note is that, much of the change is probably still due to quitting the masturbation addiction. It was such a huge part of my life for such a long time that it's hard to tell just how much it was responsible for.
