# 03-02-2026

[Ari Hoenig: How to play along with records](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xdSu4RhdAKE) Big takeaways on internalizing the music (Active Listening)
- Stick to one song a week (or two)
- Be able to sing  the melody
- Each song gets min 4 repetitions a day
  1. No play, grasp onto one part to compare with (eg piano, bass, etc)
  2. Play every other chorus
  3. Alternate with the previous
  4. Play full song
- Use each play through to either practice your own coordination exercises, or latch on and practice what they're playing
- Actively compare to what you would play

The more I think about this method, the more genius it seems. It solves so many problems:
- Repeated exposure to the same song until it's completely ingrained
- Repeated exposure to different styles
  - Brushes
  - Mallets
  - Different genres
- Interlocking with different parts
- Soloing 
- Form ingrainment
- Melody and comping exposure
