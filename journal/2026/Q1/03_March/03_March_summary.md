## Summary

<!-- Summarize the month in 1000 characters or less. -->
This month may be my first big foray into leadership. It was a painful month that tested my limits of emotional resilience. I started it on a vacation from starbucks where I mindlessly rested. I got high 3 times, overslept and overate every day, and "treated" myself over and over to sugar and fat. Moving out of this week I realized the importance of effort in my life, and how I am a person of achievement. With a mindless recovery, I went into the next week still struggling with recovering. I grew obsessed with a music theory app I'd been building with Claude as a way of circumventing pent up emotions surrounding the fallout of Jess and Amy. In the haze of this obsession, I continued overeating (or flat out poor eating), skipping workouts, skipping sleep, and committing to risky driving. I was taught in the reprieve of this obsession that this addiction grows out of fear and habitually ignoring bodily cues. The weeks proceeding this revealed deeper insights into emotional resilience, especially around others. I built a camping trip for my family and felt panicked waiting for everyone to bail and ending up their alone (only Noah's friends bailed.) I scheduled multiple lessons with Jalen and feel grateful for finally having an enthusiastic student that practices. This month ended by recognizing that the pains I feel now are all coming out from exploring connections with people in ways I never have before. Having secluded myself, I've realized my peace was a symptom of isolation wherein the only pain was loneliness, but in seeking connection I feel that I'm exposing myself as my Self for the first time. I've begun opening myself up to judgement, scrutiny, and criticism as well as love, belonging, and community. And I'm doing all of this in a way that stays true to myself. The final bits of this week have revealed that my way of coping with the pains has largely been self-destructive, and I'm moving forward intending to deal with pain by redirecting it.
