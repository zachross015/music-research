## Summary

<!-- Summarize the quarter in 1000 characters or less. -->

This quarter was filled with growth. It started out in agony (an exaggeration) as I worked hard freeing myself of the self-harming coping mechanisms that came from compulsive masturbation, eating, social media use, and really any other behavior that got in the way. When I was freed of these, it was clear to me that my reliance on them grew from aconstant overload. My focus was always so spread with competing priorities that these mechanisms were a way to keep up with the demand. By letting them go, I had to adjust my priorities so that only one thing was ever in focus at a time, and everything else was minimized. This worked so well that I can't believe I'd lived so long neglecting it. I dedicated all of April to running the half-marathon, then the next to months focusing on re-integrating myself into social media and the communities I'd been on hiatus from. Most of the effort after this was dedicated to moving from an outcome-oriented approach to one of presence, delving much deeper into stoicism and enjoying it thoroughly. All of this came to a T, making me realize I'd long held back on other avenues of my life to create the *outcome* of being a career musician, something I learned I never had control over to begin with. So instead, I moved all my goals to being based on measurable inputs within my control rather than outcomes which are only a probabiliy. That, and I allowed myself to finally aid the next biggest pain in my life: MONEY. I applied and interviewed for a shift position, made a resume to start immediately applying for private instructor positions, and began routinely posting to insta. The last component of that led me to finally facing my own social insecurities around music, connection, and lingering pain around Jess. The final month started with vacation, which left me with a peace that I started wondering how I might bring into everyday life. The answers came pretty swiftly: do more with less. Being more community minded became a larger priority, as it's more important to play with others than alone in a basement. I let go more into the present, allowing my Self to take control and getting out of the way. It's clear to me in the growth of this quarter that courage, community, and love have all become much greater priorities, and I'm looking for ways to be more self-reliant and generous to those I serve. Most importantly, I feel more present than ever before, like I understand how little control I have over where I end up, and the energy I used to devote to that can be much better devoted to living now and allowing whatever happens to come about naturally.
