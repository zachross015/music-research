I think I've started to realize why positive/negative reinforcement alike are counterintuitive. They're simple ways of casting judgement that delude the ego into believing it was somehow in control of the outcome. What eluded me recently was the question: what replaces it? I guess part of the reason that you don't try to take credit for accomplishing everything in a day is because then you also have to take credit when you do nothing

Things I'm thinking about a lot after coming back from Macon:
- when you're around cohesive people, progress is simple. Playing with Turner and Jackson makes it obvious that the person makes all the difference. Playing with people who actually want the challenge of Limelight makes limelight happen. And having the excitement/morale is a huge factor. Perfection isn't nearly as important there. And we're more willing to take risks.
- What is the role of commenting on their performances? In the stoic lens, is it only important that I improve my side of the game?
- I don't need to be a "leader" in the sense of commanding them. Only in the sense of showing up each time with the standard I want to hold and having the courage to treat our group rehearsals as I would my own (with deep focus)
- Feel much more courageous and confident after being around them. Like I AM capable of great things
- Need to let go of the commentary

What I noticed on the floor:
- all of the initiative to take action fades when I'm in ego territory, and having played with my friends who kinda conflated my ego made me less generous. I have to actively work back to a generous state
- Momo takes a lot of credit for others, which is why I think it's hard to work under them, but it's my judgement alone of such a behavior that makes it hard to be me around them
- The principles of stoicism form a closed loop of improvement. If you learn to live without judgement, you can be whoever you want. Most importantly, you can be yourself, because by not judging others you're not judging yourself. Courage on the other hand is the ability to be yourself externally (judgement internally) 
- to get more students, I have to take the initiative.

With all that in mind, it's best I see myself not as a leader of this group or see the arduous task of moving reality, but instead live completely in the moment and make the best of what's in front of me. Contentment with my lot and all. I think that's the terms I'm growing more comfortable with, and it's a strength I've never felt.

There's an undeniable urge to take credit for all of it. To put my fingers into the lives of Jackson and Turner and turn them into puppets, as if that were even an option. And I see then why Momo's approach feels so harmful: it discounts the efforts of everyone else involved. This practice was a success because all of us individually put in meaningful effort. 

I see more and more, the power of a network, even just communicating a want broadly gets broadcast by people in your network. That increases luck, but to be a part of it is to leave your ego at the door. The intimidating part of that is that it opens the possibility to achieve whatever you want (possibility, not guarantee,) and so the question becomes what are you going to do with it?

The advanced process of art is being able to adapt on the fly, converting distraction (in the form of mistakes) into a brand new meaning. Developing an ear is about being able to recognize spontaneous parts not as mistakes, but as variations that you can give meaning

All of that said, I feel strong. Confident. Like I'm headed in the right direction, and people around me see it. I feel proud for having messaged Turner and Jackson about all this, then again to meet, and for how prepared I showed up. But I don't know what I can possibly take credit for beyond that, and yet I feel powerful as if I was the one that put everything together, learned all the parts, and played them. But maybe there's truth to that. Not me as in my Self, but me as in the collective universe that Turner and Jackson are apart of. And really, thats what i mean by the power of a network. The power that i, as the part of the collective consciousness, could have if i become part of it.
