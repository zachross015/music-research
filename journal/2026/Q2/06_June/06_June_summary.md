## Summary

<!-- Summarize the month in 1000 characters or less. -->

This month was one of rapid growth and rapid changes. I began the month on a family vacation, wherein I let go and still managed to spend most of the time kayaking, seeing the sunrise, running, cooking healthy foods, writing music, and spending time with loved ones all at the behest of my ego. What was funny was the entire time, I was worried about how little I wanted those things recently, and yet I went after all them anyways. Following this week, I began questioning the superflous activities I'd dedicated myself to. Having achieved such deep peace on a vacation made me see that this was something that could be brought to everyday life, and I started asking how. This drove me deeper into studies of presence, stoicism, and Adlerian psychology, with the major learning being how much I'd tried to control the outcomes of the life I was living rather than living it moment to moment and allowing greatness to arise as a natural consequence of joys. This alone resulted in a major improvement in my viewpoint. I began seeing what flow, deep focus, actually felt like, and how good it felt in moments I was freed of my ego. Not only that, but how community comes so naturally to someone who is always present. My continued experience with letting go and finding spontaneous moments for meditation greatly improved my communication skills. I stopped to talk with Jennifer and Drew at one point. I helped organize a rehearsal with Nomad. I helped put together Father's day (even with the painful money problems.) Best of all, I've improved my practice greatly by seeing the role of focus and Self 2's natural learning process. All in all, I've begun seeing life works its best when I get out of the way of myself. This can be said for writing music, talking with people, meditating, or literally anything else. And that there is a natural learning process that takes place by solely visualizing and observing, and that we are all small parts of a collective concious whose language is communication. And all in all, I've found now that the best results happen not by micromanaging every step, but by picturing a goal clearly then getting out of the way. It's in doing this that courage is not drained with the tedium of every task.
