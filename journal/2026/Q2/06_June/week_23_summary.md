## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->

Vacation last week, so this one will be a two in one.

Started with the end of jazz fest and a deep contemplation on the nature of connection, specifically isolation versus community. This was something I thought alot about the two weeks. I'd been so focused on all the people that didn't come, including my family, that I'd neglected the people who did show, Tate and JJ. The rest of the first week was the beginning of a rest period. I had a show on Mondy and a potential camping trip on Tuesday/Wednesday, but Mu and Maddie got into a fight on Monday which, combined with the increasingly inclement weather, gave me good reason to bail on the trip. I spent the majority of that week resting and permitting indulgence. Coke, Minecraft, and Youtube. It was much needed, and a great acknowledgement of the burnout I felt. Still, what agonized me the most was my disconnect from nature and how I'd not desired it as I once did.

This theme carried onto the next week, where my family vacation was a period of letting go. I ate like shit, played video games, and masturbated a couple times, but fortunately I did none of these things to avoid and none out of compulsion. Well, maybe I did compulsively eat a donut or two. That aside, I felt alot of love that week. Developing my security has made it easier to get along with family, to stick up for myself where necessary, and to be true. I also felt more at peace than I had in a long time, maybe ever. I listened to music on the whole ride up. I read my books. I felt like I was finally starting to settle into myself and pursue the things I enjoyed without worrying what others thought. Everyday I woke up early and went to bed early, I cooked my own breakfasts and lunch, I'd ran once, meditated everyday, kayaked most days, and spent a plethora of time with the family. But all the while, I stayed present and stayed with msyelf. While there was discourse with a friend's rape rumor, struggles staying focused on financial objectives, and conflict between family members, I managed to stay at peace and out of it all. Great job! I even wrote two songs! Probably one of the best realizations this week is that I'm an Artist > Musician > Drummer, and that when given the space to breathe all I end up doing is creating. It makes me wonder how I might begin to carry the peace of this vacation into everyday life.
