## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->

This week was one of realizing more lessons on the Self. I started off the week recording a music video with Parking Garage, where I volunteered to be an editor. I'd been reading The Courage to Be Disliked, which made me begin thinking more of how I could become community minded, and felt that was a good start. Throughout the week, this revelation continued blossoming. There is such a thing of letting go of the need for the relationship with the Self, and in fact I'd been plagued the past few years with a notion that many of my problems evolved from focuing entirely too much on...my problems. I began having this revelation with writing music, where I wrote best when I stopped getting in the way and trying to write and instead just sat down and allowed whatever I was feeling to come through freely, with emphasis on not taking credit for it and instead just enjoying it. The evolution of this thought process began hitting my social life, where I saw how I'd used my fears and problems to create distance between myself and my family. This reached a climax during a tubing trip with my family, where I felt antagonized by the history of Ross family fun time and engineered a panic to keep me from talking with them about music. But the lesson I'd learnt over and over was that everything gets done better and faster when I let it happen and maintain focus on the present, letting go of the outcome in favor of spending quality time with the material.

In an effort to summarize learnings:
- Vertical relationships create superiority/inferiority complexes, while horizontal ones make it easier to connect
- Self-Reliance is one of the highest priorities in Adlerian psychology, and explains many of my insecurities
- Learning and living all happen more cleanly when you (ego) stop interfering with them and instead allow yourself to enjoy it
