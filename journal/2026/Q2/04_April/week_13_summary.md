## Summary

It was an emotionally eventful week.

> A theory I have: the depression tends to start from an overloaded schedule. Which leads to
> - Neglecting sleep OR other destructive coping mechanisms 
> - Missing recovery
> - Pushing too hard to make up for lost time
> - Developing injuries
> - Developing fear associated with the injuries
> - Neglecting The Practice
> - Frustration with self for not wanting to practice these things and continued "soothing" in destructive ways
> - Spiral

I spent the week seeing the main source of burnout that's been popping up in my life again and again: the constant divide of attention. Each of my different areas of life keep inflating over time until I'm so deep in "task debt" that nothing is achieveable. Along with the change in diet, whose adjustment caused many energy problems throughout the week, and the flatline period of the masturbation withdrawals, I spent the week in a dense fog. It was in this fog I realized the inhuman ask that is my priorities, and that as a response to stress and discomfort, I inflated the importance of committing more time and resources to each and every direction just to get out of the fog. The Stoic in me realized later that this only made the fog worse and led to the same burnout I'd seen over and over. I've fallen over and over again into the turbulence of day-to-day. This has made progress difficult everywhere as little commitment has been made to the development of any activity. I traced the origin of this habit to three sources:
1. School. There's absolutely little focus taught in school, as every course is taught in a way that competes with the focus of other courses, and all are given equal emphasis. It keeps you in a mindset of having to do everything and focus on nothing.
2. Fantasy. It's another avenue of fantasy, floundering around and waiting for someone to control every aspect of my life. Needing external guidance to limit what I do and waiting for someone to take the agency from me. 
3. Home. Where school started the issue, my parents continued controlling and making these decisions for me. 
Sitting in Milledgeville after an emotional breakdown, I was finally able to see the pains I was struggling with as having originated in college. Somehow related to the focus issues were issues revolving around putting effort into events I lead. I looked back on college as being my first time making actual decisions, many of which I regret now, and pains around JEss and Angelo resurfacing.
