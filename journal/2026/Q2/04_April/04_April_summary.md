## Summary

<!-- Summarize the month in 1000 characters or less. -->
This month was the height of all my withdrawls. Everything with masturbation entered a flatline phase, I'd cut out my remaining dopamine addictions, and half-marathon training came in at full force. The limits of these intensities revealed the breaking point of my coping mechanisms and I suffered. But in growth fashion, I sat with the evidence until I recognized that these coping mechanisms existed in the first place to keep up with my disjoint and ever-growing priorities in every field (a leftover habit from childhood). Limiting my focus to one item a quarter, and drawing everything else in the MEM rotation back to a bare minimum freed up most of my time and made it easier to attend to my goals dilligently. It also made prioritizing the steps to achieving a real goal (eg running the half marathon) more attainable because I could finally distinguish between the most important action for the moment. The rest of the month was dedicated to refining this method of focus, which led me into the depths of Stoicism. One other branch of suffering I was resolving this month was the macrophilia. While I'm not certain it's gone for good, I spent alot of time delving into the ways I've waited for someone to take hold of me, take responsibility of me, and take away all of my control. Eg, treat me like an accessory or like I'm invisible. Investigating this invisibility cloak led me to realize how little I'd taken responsibility for my decisions, and much of this stems from the little autonomy I was given up until college where I began making bad decisions as my first foray into decision making. The combination of these two threads led me to a deeper realization: an overfocus and over-dependence on outcomes. Returning to the present and choosing every moment every day to pursue virtue, curiosity, and align my decisions with these and my goal has led to instant returns in the forms of social risk/growth, inner security, and better decision making.
