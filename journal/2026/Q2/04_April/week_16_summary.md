## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->

Started this week feeling a relief from the flatline and withdrawls I'd been suffering. I'd rearranged my room to be an actual bedrooom and dug deeper into Marcus Aurelius's Meditations. The stoic principles began shaking me when I realized my way of handling emotions, stress, and even goals lacked any presence. They still attempted resolving emotions by sitting with them until they went away or sinking into an overthinking loop to understand and diagnose the pain, both of which resulted in larger pain due to the time sink. And with goals, I began realizing the outcome-oriented approach I held with all of them has never worked, as it relegates the joy of the goal to the outcome instead of the process. I sat with this the rest of the week, understanding more of the immediate changes I would make if I allowed myself to grow into a professional as a symptom of curiosity and delegating the coaching to...an actual coach. Ended the week with finishing a half-marathon, investigating curiosity more dilligently, and going out of my social comfort zone to stand up for myself in front of family, asking Nikki to hang out, and being the hype man during the half.

### Notable Quote

Everything with Stoicism is brushing up against latent insecurities and drawing fear inside. My biggest fear is that without all these plans, I have no control over the future. Almost like I've been using structure as a shield, something to legitimize my practice when people ask. But if I change my approach to one that holds no promises for the future and instead diverts all my energy towards making the most of each day...I could see how life changing that would be, but I worry about what that means for my practice. There's a genuine curiosity which I fight. I'm having a really hard time with it. I'm also overthinking it.

It's like...it's like I'm giving up. Giving up control, or admitting I'd never had it. For so long I'd dreamed of a better future...but it's gotten me nowhere. I've had plans of being "the best drummer in the world," but...idk. When I grasp the idea that it's only supposed to be about showing up as the person you want to be everyday, then it feels like I've wasted so much time. Because I don't need a part time job to chase curiosity. And I'm not hungry enough to chase gigs. The obvious path forward would be to immediately move somewhere the work existed and make do. Get to New York or Nashville and immerse myself. Or even finding work around here. But instead, I'm lamenting and toiling away in my basement. It doesn't feel like I deserve to sit still or be curioius or take a break. I've gotten better, but still so little has changed.

I understand the depth of presence now. That if you commit to virtues and investigate curiosity liberally, or otherwise let joy guide the path every day, then you are likely to have a happy life. That its about the process, not the outcome. The labor, not the fruit. But I've become do dependent on these outcomes, if nothing else than to prevent insecurity, that they now are an obsession. Ther are desires. Prisons. Familiarities. Attachments. I'm stepping into an unknown where I may never become the best drummer in the world. I may never get married. I may never leave Starbucks. I may never move out. I may never have a carrer in music. I might die alone tomorrow. All I can hope to do is show up.
