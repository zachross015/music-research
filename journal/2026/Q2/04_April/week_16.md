I understand it all deeper this morning. Becoming an honest person isn't about making an attempt to be more honest, but instead from moment to moment and decision to decision making the honest choice. The same applies to any virtue.

Having a goal is only a means to making decisions in the present. Insecurity only comes from a mind stuck in the past, the future, or in someone else's head. The more you can bring your mind to move only with your body and not with the other 4 dimensions, the more peace you feel. But much of this means growing a resilience to the discomforts of the body.

I think it all means to make decisions in the present that reflect your values. Attachment to a goal or identity, especially one inconceivably far away, only serves to create friction and reinforce an outdated identity.

It's not about becoming someone. It's about BEING someone. Choosing every moment to make decisions which align with an overall set of values and a goal, but no attachment to either.

I have experienced this time and time again. Envisioning and holding myself to a future in such a way that changes constantly and shifts priorities constantly. When the dream disappears, I feel lost looking for the motivation to reach it, which makes certain I'd attached to the dream.

In that vein, growing as a musician doesn't come from a solitary practice. It plays a part, but only so much as to explore the depths of curiosity and refine the craft. Trying to say "this is the drummer I want to become" is an attempt at controlling an outcome. The much better alternative is to search for work, let what's there guide practice, and let everything else be guided by curiosity and creation.
