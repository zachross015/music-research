## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->
Started out the week in exhaustion, which didn't ever seem to go away throughout it. I've been hanging onto the vacation on the horizon for sometime, waiting for it to come to have reprieve. I battled alot of social anxiety through the week, trying to find time to recuperate but never quite getting back to a baseline. The return to social media has me battling with insecurities that have only recently become clear, like those surrounding competition, influence, attending events with people, staying true to myself in all these scenarios, etc. After losing the barista championship, I was reminded of the humbling nature of competition. It sent me spinning for a day as I realized at a deeper level the distance between where I was and where I wanted to be in many things: chess, coffee, music, career, etc. It created a deeper awareness that not only was I not where I wanted to be, but I don't have the means of getting there alone. That, and these high stress scenarios require the same recovery as a half-marathon, yet I was not making room for them. Ended the week with JazzFest, an incredible festival where I once again felt isolated by the lack of people that I cared for. It made me look inward, feeling once again like no one cares about the things I care about or the things I want, and like something was wrong with me for it. While Nate Smith was truly inspirational, I felt sad at my continuing inability to inspire others to come out, or make an event like this seem appealing, and felt jealous of everyone else there. It drove me back into an isolated state of mind, which led me to not tipping, getting mad at others in traffic, and retreating to a self-titled band where I didn't have to count on others unreliability.
