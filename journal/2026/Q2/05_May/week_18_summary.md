## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->
The Week started recovering from grief. I'd been so tangled up in the guilt I still felt from everything with Jess and Angelo until I came to the realization that I've continued holding onto it for no reason other than to keep it alive. Letting this go allowed me to center back on practice, social media, and growing my life. I applied to the Shift position at SBUX and talked to Kendall about it. Then, I DMed several members of the music community, asking for advice and even complimenting them. I put my ego aside to listen to Drew's new album (which was awesome) along with several other musicians, and while I struggled going into it I began realizing the boon of listening to more and more music, and the reasons I struggled with live performances came from how little I'd supported others (yet expected them to support me.) more importantantly, I went out of my comfort zone to invite many people to the Isra gig. Although only 3 showed, it came with the realization that I'd been too outcome oriented with inviting crowds to shows, targeting audiences who aren't in the habit of coming to live music. And that doing so with this level of expectation (especially from the perspective of income) is too much pressure to succeed in a low stakes environment. The other side of this coin is a tendency to undermine myself, actively convincing people not to come while also desparately hoping they do. I ended the week realizing the nature of insecurity in my decision making. The endless repetition of my own music/playing is a form of obsession, seeking flaws and tinkering with performance where there is no professional feedback. The same happens with social media. It amounts to a fixation on self-criticality as an attempt to adjust levers with no ability to tell which one is broken and no way to fix it when its found. It also acts as a distraction by creating a false sense of urgency to immediate issues which don't recur.
