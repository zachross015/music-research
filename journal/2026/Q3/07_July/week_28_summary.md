## Summary

<!-- Summarize the week in 1000 characters or less. -->

This was a tumultuous week. My family was an Italy, and I was watching the dogs. I'd gone into the week with a set of goals: make a resume, contact Danny about assistance getting a job, apply to 5 stores a day, announce to the world I was taking new students, and overall generate an exit plan for Starbucks. This was a hefty set of goals, overall setting the framework for a major life change. While I'd initially thought these tasks to be simple, they all came with heavy emotional baggage. The dust was kicked up and a storm entered my mind. I'd spent the week agonizing, not necessarily over the tasks so much as the social atmosphere I needed to see these through. It took me until saturday to have the courage to message Danny AND post about opening my lessons practice up. Even then, I threw my phone in a bin immediately after and still have yet to recover it. The same tension appeared with applying for Musicologie positions: I'd created my resume and cover letter and after submitting them distanced myself from the task. It's still not entirely clear why these have been such painful actions, but digging around they seem to all be rooted in a vulnerability around architecting change in this world. I'm moving from a participant in the lives around me to someone who helps shape them, and leading in this regard has surfaced latent fears around connection and consistency. For instance, I'll distance myself from someone who gets too close to me, or refuse to become a regular at Peach's out of fear that they may actually begin to know me (especially the cute barista). But even now, I see the deeper power of intentions and visualizations, because in spite of the pain I've faced my body has adjusted. It (or God) made all of these things happen, and subsequently forced me into recovery when it needed it. It's only been my distrust, or urgency, that has exacerbated the pain. Life is about to change, I am repotting, and I can expect this to be a stressful process, but it need not be painful.
